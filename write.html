Flowers on the graveyard.

Flowers to whom I care In the graveyard of this despairr Circling with butterflies of gold For no rooms to a lonesome soul.

Shedding tears in the midnight Had to keep my only paradise No sweet melancholy nor rainbows For it's always a gloomy weather.

I see the raging black storm In a deep wave of ocean eyes Couldn't move nor couldn't breathe For I am suffocated underneath.

Had no saccharine nor rainbows In vastness of sorrow and angst Painted dark scars in core of mine For I am alive yet seemingly dead.

Living in tragics.

Hauling out from the lows, convulsing in the cold with sunken eyes, have I gone too far? The dim had me blunted and dampened, perhaps, this is how it should have always been. And withal, I have been plagued by fuming hurricanes, that seemed to have no end or so I thought.

Still yet, I have tried to brawl against the wind while the pallid, menacing ash has taken its untamed fall. It does kill and kill, like a black rain dawdling and invading through pale bones. And yes, it really does murder the fragile ones.
